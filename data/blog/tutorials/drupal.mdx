## What is Drupal?

Drupal is a content management system. Several websites and applications you use every day are built using this technology. In addition to its great standard features, Drupal offers excellent performance, excellent security, and easy content authoring. However, what sets it apart is its flexibility. Modularity is one of its defining features. By utilizing its tools, you can create the flexible, structured content needed for dynamic web experiences.

Also, it is a good choice for integrating digital frameworks. It can be extended with any number of thousands of add-ons. Modules can extend drupal. Themes allow you to customize the way your content is displayed. Distributions are pre-packaged Drupal bundles you can use as starter kits. These components can be mixed and matched to enhance Drupal's core capabilities. Alternatively, Drupal can be integrated with external services and other applications in your environment. Drupal is unique in its ability to integrate with external services and other applications.

Drupal is an open-source project. It may be downloaded, used, modified, and shared by anyone. It is based on principles such as collaboration, globalism, and innovation. The GNU General Public License (GPL) governs its distribution. No license fees are ever charged. Drupal is always free.

## Drupal AVIF support

According to Github Discussions, Drupal has WebP and AVIF support since October 2021.
